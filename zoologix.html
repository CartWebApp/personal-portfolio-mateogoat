Overview

As an intern for the Fresno Chaffee Zoo, I worked on the enrichment application named Zoologix, specifically the logging enrichment portion. Previously, FCZ’s current enrichment tracking application did not allow them to log enrichments on the go because it is not available on mobile. Zoologix fixes that and makes it easier for animal curators to log enrichment activities than ever before.

Concepts Learned

  • Cross-functional Teamwork: I worked with people of different skill sets and demographics, allowing for different inputs on what could be the best solution for our client. As a collaborative effort, we managed to minimize the number of steps an animal curator has to take in order to log a daily enrichment.
  • Complex Figma Prototyping: I learned how to use variants for many of the functions inside the hi-fi prototype and learned how to use auto layout to make the development process way more efficient.

Problem Solutions

  • Little Time to Complete Work: This project was very fast-paced, and we had to move through the development process accordingly. Because of that, it taught me the importance of collaboration outside of class to make sure our client’s product is submitted on time.
  • Many Pieces of Information: This user flow is not an easy task to solve, having many steps required for animal curators to log all of the enrichment data for successful management of their animals. To solve this, we decided to go with a drop-down design to allow for many pieces of information on such a small screen size.

Zoologix in different screen sizes, optimized by Mateo Siechert

Habits of Mind

  • Analytical Thinking: As for all of my projects, I had to keep track of the statistics on how many animals these animal curators have to manage. After we did interviews with our client’s employees, we learned it is quite difficult at the moment to log all of the enrichments for all of these animals in a day. To solve this problem, we have a search feature inside of the application that makes it easy to find a certain animal or animals to log an enrichment on.
