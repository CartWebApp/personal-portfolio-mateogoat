Overview

MVS prevents a user from procrastinating on their schoolwork by providing all the resources to stay on track with assignments. This application allows students to sync their Q Student Connect account to check their grades and connect not just one, but two school email accounts to sync Google Classroom assignments in one place.

Concepts Learned

  • Responsive Design: A requirement for this device was to design this application for not just mobile devices, but also including desktop computers and tablets, all of which students may use with MVS.
  • Usability Testing: An important user experience rule you must follow is to receive feedback from users who may use your product and then update the product if there is something that may be an issue. I have interviewed professional user experience designers, and I received important feedback on what to improve upon.

Problem Solutions

Developer Bias: As a high school student while developing the application, I had some bias into what might be the best answer to student procrastination. To solve this issue I interviewed and surveyed potential students that may use this product and receive their feedback on what could be a great technique to solve student procrastination as a whole.

Habits of Mind

  • Key Communication: I spoke with my instructor heavily on if there were issues with the CSS styling, or if I had equal work being done with my team. This was an important step in working as a team, as you may need to tell your employer if there is any issues during development.
  • Team Leadership: Holding the weight of this projects proves to not just my peers, but also future employers that I am capable of working more than the bare minimum; most of tis webpage was designed and coded by me, with assistance from my peers alongside my instructor if I struggled on anything.
