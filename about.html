Life at CART

Originally, my first lab choice in my junior year of high school was the Web App program at CART, but I instead received my second choice, UX Design. One year later I am officially in the lab I originally wanted. This lab originally I was quite nervous about as after taking UX Design I realized there are a lot more technical skills required compared to last year. The first project that really challenged me was creating PawPal, a pet wellness tracker that allows users to keep in check major necessities to keep their animal healthy and well. However, it was the first project that required me to understand CSS styling in web design, which I struggled with originally. I asked for assistance from both my instructor and my peers that really brought the light of day to style a website both on desktop and mobile. With practice exercises that my instructor gave me, I learned the many different techniques a developer can use when positioning information on a webpage on different screen types, which is called media queries. I also learned that if you use many different CSS selectors instead of one for everything, it is easier to modify a certain attribute on the website instead of selecting everything with a class for example. The EECU Budget Calculator project taught me how to implement chart.js and Fetch API to allow for visuals on a user’s monthly expenses in real time and having the website receive an array of data from a separate website entirely for the career selector. I had used my collaboration skills in this class, especially during the EECU Budget Calculator project where many of my peers took a crack at adding their own iteration of code onto my design. There are two projects left for my Web App class at CART, one being an adventure project requiring heavy ideation and finding techniques on how to implement a puzzle portion of the game where a user attempts to put a shape in the correct hole. Our team used drag and drop API to allow a user move an object on the website itself, making our puzzle function perfectly. With my two years in the technology sector at CART both in UX Design and Web App, I have had six projects under my belt that have brought me experience to say the least. I am very well suited for a job in this field after I graduate high school. To further my experience, I am majoring in this field with Communication Design at CSU Monterey Bay to then receive internships for even more hands-on experience than what I already have
