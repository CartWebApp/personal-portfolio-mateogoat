Overview

I had an internship with a startup supercar company and designed a car configurator application that allows a user to customize the exterior and interior of their vehicle, including wheel color, body color, sound systems, and allows them to submit their contact information to further the order process.

Concepts Learned

  • User Personas: A crucial piece you must do as a UX designer is to put yourself in the potential user’s shoes and think about how they might think. I had a user persona created that was based on a wealthy person and how their priorities may be completely different from those of the developers themselves.

Problem Solutions

  • Non-Equal Teamwork: A very big issue at the beginning of the development process was my peers, who did not want to complete their work in a timely manner, or even do it at all. My role was a mobile UX designer, and I had to split between my web team and my mobile team, and ended up with a much better product in the end.
  • Initial Design Choices: In the beginning, I had a completely different idea when it came to how the car configurator would be laid out. However, after some usability testing, I learned that having the configurator in a landscape format allowed for not just less clutter, but an easier-to-use UI that a potential buyer of a supercar would use.

Habits of Mind

  • Ethical Consideration: During this project, I looked beyond not just what the problem was, but also why the problem is. During that thought process, I realized this struggle with the non-equal teamwork along with my peers happened for a reason, and that it was a lesson to me that it will happen all throughout life.
  • Analytical Thinking: As with anything related to vehicles, statistics are important to follow. We did a competitive audit to find out what other supercar companies are doing in order to find what was truly feasible for Trion Supercars and their potential future customers.
