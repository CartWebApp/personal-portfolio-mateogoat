Here is a snapshot of some of the showcase reflection by our team

Overview

After presenting to thousands at the 2026 Center for Advanced Research and Technology (CART) Showcase, I was tasked with reflecting on what we can improve for a presentation as big as this one. This was a special portion for improvement, even though we placed third for best presentation out of 1,600 students at CART.

Concepts Learned

  • Public Speaking: Presenting what our team created with Zoologix was quite nerve-racking at the beginning, but I later realized you can use that fear as a reason to persevere in making an incredible presentation. I now have way more confidence as a public speaker, but it is not just confidence that makes a good presentation. What makes a really good presentation is the knowledge of what you and your peers created, and if you do not know what you created, you should not present that work to others.
  • Practice Outside Work Hours: The key difference between our team and others during Showcase was us practicing our presentation as a team off campus to perfect any usage of slubs when we actually present to others. Alongside doing this allows you to feel less stressed prior to the day the presentation comes along.

Challenge Solutions

Presenting Nervousness: Being nominated for the best presentation group of all the classes at CART was a major role to fill for the entire technology lab sector of the school. There was a lot of nervousness between my teammates and me, but we as a group realized that this was an honor to receive this nomination. We then practiced heavily to make sure it is perfected to the best of our ability.

  • Flexibility: I kept in mind that all of my teammates have things going on in their lives, which may cause them to miss meetings outside of class. I had the flexibility to work around people’s schedules to make sure we could have a perfect presentation.
  • Openness: During showcase practice, there were still many things we could improve upon when it comes to our presentation. Our instructors gave honest feedback on ways to perfect our speaking and its fluency. Instead of snubbing it away, our team took this feedback and practiced heavily on how we can implement that into our presentation, and it paid off at the end.
